Do you want to be part of making better patient information?

Then, Please read the following information and contact us to participate in a PPI study

Hello,
We invite you to be part of creating a tool for making better Patient Information Leaflets (PILs). Currently, there is no standard assessment on the leaflets quality and many professionals believe that the leaflets have no impact on the patientsâ€™ decision because they are not read.

Why is this study important?
Because we seek to provide you with an objective system to increase the quality of PILs, by identifying tools that could help you and other principal investigators (PIs) to find specific issues in an structured format.

What is our idea?
To create a computational system that gives PIs an objective assessment on their leaflets and a structured output for their Patient and Public Involvement (PPI) feedback which links the comments to specific sections in the PIL.
We include several techniques employed in analysing text on the Web, like employing quantitative metrics to analyse the sentences, using sentiment analysis to highlight problematic sections in the document and similarity metrics to identify past comments that may be relevant to your text (demo at wppi.soton.ac.uk/demos).

What will you do?
You will be asked to provide at least one PIL developed in the last 2 years for a low-risk trial. The PIL must be in English and address adults with capacity. If you are selected to be part of the study, you will receive feedback on your leaflet from a PPI group and then asked to revise it.

How are the participants selected?
We have opted for an exploratory randomised study in which each group may receive a report based on a particular technique in addition to the PPI group feedback. Given the economic constrains in our research funding and to reduce the systematic differences in the study, only one PPI group of 10 people will be created. Thus, the PILs selected will address target populations with similar demographics.

What do I need to participate?
You need to fill in a form at wppi.soton.ac.uk/authors, provide us with at least 1 PIL for a low-risk trial developed in the last 2 years in a text format (doc, docx, txt, odt), and be able to revise it after receiving the feedback via the website in a period of about 3 weeks.

How long would it take?
We expect reviewing the online feedback on your leaflet and making a revision would not take you any longer than your normal process.

Will my data be anonymous?
Yes, we will not record any information that can be used to identify you. The leaflets will be anonymised by deleting the names of organizations, people and places. Should you prefer it you can send an anonymised version of your leaflet(s).

How will my PIL be used?
We will provide an anonymised version of your PIL to a PPI group (10 members) to be reviewed. The PPI group will employ the Cloze procedure, extended EQIP guideline and the reviewer Web platform (WPPI) (wppi.soton.ac.uk/demos) to review your PIL. This information will be used to assess changes between the original and revised versions of your document. You will receive the PPI group comments via the WPPI platform.

How can I join?
You can join by filling in the application form on our website wppi.soton.ac.uk or by contacting the principal investigator Fernando Santos at fss1g15@soton.ac.uk.
