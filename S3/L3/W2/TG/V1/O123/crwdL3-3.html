The following list contains some tips on how to revise medical literature to make it more understandable for the general public.

1.- Avoid wordiness:
"At this point in time"
->
"now"
2.- Delete redundant words:
"The reason why"
->
"The reason"
3.- Look out for "Did not" and "Do not":
"Did not succeed"
->
"failed"
4.- Avoid "It", "There is" and "There are" as sentence openers:
"There are serious consequences in failing to take your medication"
->
"Failing to take your medication can have serious consequences"
5.- Delete needless "To be":
"The results were shown to be conclusive"
->
"The results were conclusive"
6.- Reword weak and wordy verbs:
"is in conflict with"
->
"conflicts"
7.- Delete needless "That" and "Which":
"This is a problem that is easy to correct"
->
"This problem is easy to correct"
8.- Don't use a noun when a verb can be used:
"Give consideration to taking part in this study"
->
"Consider participating in this study"
9.- Avoid repetition:
"In trauma victims, breathing is restored by artificial respiration. Techniques of artificial respiration include mouth-to-mouth respiration and mouth to nose respiration"
->
"In trauma victims, breathing is restored by artificial respiration, either mouth-to-mouth or mouth-to-nose."
